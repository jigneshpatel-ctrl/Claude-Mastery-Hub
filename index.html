Prompting Mastery

Tips & Tricks

Techniques that consistently produce better, more reliable outputs from Claude.

Fundamentals (Learn These First)

01

Role + Context + Goal in every prompt

Tell Claude who to be, what context applies, and exactly what you need. You are a senior backend engineer at a fintech startup. We're building payment reconciliation. Explain idempotency keys with production-ready pseudocode.

02

Chain-of-Thought on complex tasks

Add Think step by step before answering. Forces Claude to reason through logic first — the single highest-leverage technique for complex problems. Quality difference is immediate and dramatic.

03

Specify output format explicitly

Say exactly what you want: Return as JSON, Use a markdown table, Numbered steps, Flowing prose, no headers. Claude mirrors format unless directed — always direct it.

04

XML tags for complex prompts

Separate sections with <context>, <task>, <constraints>, <format> tags. Claude parses structured prompts with significantly higher accuracy than plain paragraphs.

05

Audience + depth specification

Tell Claude who reads this: Explain for a non-technical PM vs Assume senior SRE audience. This single change dramatically affects technical depth, terminology, and assumed knowledge.

06

Negative constraints

Tell Claude what NOT to do: Don't use third-party services, Avoid theoretical examples, Skip the introduction paragraph. As powerful as positive instructions — often more so for controlling output shape.

Power Techniques (Intermediate → Advanced)

07

Few-shot prompting (examples first)

Give 1-2 examples of your ideal output before asking the real question. The most reliable way to control output style, format, and depth. Claude infers your pattern instantly — format, tone, length, everything.

08

Challenge your own assumptions

After any design or plan: Play a skeptical senior engineer. What are the 5 biggest weaknesses? What would break first at 10x scale? What assumptions might not hold? Surfaces blind spots better than formal reviews.

09

Multiple options + trade-offs → recommendation

Give 3 approaches with trade-offs for performance, complexity, and cost — then push: Given my context is X and constraint is Y, which do you specifically recommend and why? Forces concrete advice.

10

Flag uncertainty explicitly

Add: Rate your confidence (High/Medium/Low) per section. Flag anything uncertain. Critical before acting on technical outputs. Claude will mark the gaps. Verify those parts independently.

11

Simulate stress tests

Walk through what breaks as we go from current load to 10x to 100x. At each level, what fails first and why? Claude identifies architectural weaknesses before they hit production. Use before every major design review.

12

Extended Thinking mode

Enable in claude.ai or add Take time to reason through this thoroughly. Show your reasoning process before answering. Produces qualitatively different, deeper answers for complex decisions — not just more words.

13

Projects for persistent context

Set up a Claude Project once with your role, tech stack, and company context. Every session inherits it. You stop repeating context. Highest ROI one-time setup — takes 10 minutes, saves hours every week.

14

Iterative depth drilling

First response is always a draft. Push: Too generic. Make it specific to a B2B SaaS with 50k daily transactions, a 3-person backend team, and PostgreSQL on AWS RDS. Each push adds a layer of depth.

What Kills Output Quality

Anti-Patterns to Avoid

A small set of repeatable mistakes caps most users at 60% quality. Eliminating these doubles output quality with zero extra effort.

⚠️

Read this first

Before rating any Claude response as poor — audit your prompt against these anti-patterns first. Most "Claude failures" are actually prompt failures.

✗

Vague open-ended prompts

"Tell me about payments" → Wikipedia overview. Always specify: which aspect, what depth, what format, for what purpose. Vague in = generic out. Every single time.

✗

Trusting without verification

Claude is confident even when wrong. Never ship code, data, or decisions based solely on Claude's output. Validate technical facts, APIs, regulations, and numbers independently.

✗

No context between sessions

Claude has zero memory between chats. Use Projects for persistent context, or always re-establish role + constraints at the start of every session. This is non-negotiable for quality.

✗

Accepting the first response as final

Real value comes from iteration. Push back: "Make this specific to X", "What edge cases are missing?", "Give me the opposite view." First responses are always drafts, never finals.

✗

Overloading one prompt

5 questions in one message → Claude addresses all superficially. One clear goal per interaction. Use follow-ups for depth. Separate concerns into separate messages.

✗

Not asking for edge cases

Claude defaults to the happy path. For any design or flow, always add: What could go wrong? What failure modes am I missing? What are the edge cases I haven't considered?

✗

Accepting "it depends" without drilling

When Claude hedges, force a concrete answer: Given my context is X and constraint is Y, which specific approach do you recommend and why? Never let it off the hook with non-answers.

✗

Using Claude as a search engine for current info

Claude has a knowledge cutoff. For current regulations, recent API changes, or fast-moving topics — use built-in web search or verify with official docs. Don't trust training data for time-sensitive facts.
